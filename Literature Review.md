Majority of the literature review concerns [[Solutions to UWB SAR]], with some on [[UWB SAR Challenges]]. The fundamental theory on [[Synthetic Aperture Radar]] is found in the classical texts. This forms a large basis for [[Phase 1]].

### SAR fundamental theory

***Tutorial on Synthetic Aperture Radar***
- Well cited paper on the basics on synthetic aperture radar.

### UWB Specifics 

***Issues in UltraWideband, Widebeam SAR Image Formation***
- Older paper which provides some brief overview of the challenges seen in UWB SAR.

***A Comprehensive Overview on UWB Radar: Applications, Standards, Signal Processing Techniques, Datasets, Radio Chips, Trends and Future Research Directions***
- Presents some of the fundamental UWB theory, basics and implementations.

### SAR processing techniques

***A Residual Range Cell Migration Correction Algorithm for SAR based on Low-Frequency Fitting*** 
- As range resolution becomes finer, such as the cases of large bandwidth, RCM exceeds a single range resolution cell.
- Strategies include estimating the azimuth phase error term and calculating residual RCM from the estimated RCM phase error.
- States some current methods of RCM correction.
- Presents the formulation of range migration.

***RDAnet: A Deep Learning Based Approach for Synthetic Aperture Radar Image Formation:***
- Presents an alternative approach to traditional signal processing algorithms by processing an end to end deep learning pipeline.

***Sparse Synthetic Aperture Radar Imaging From Compressed Sensing and Machine Learning: Theories, Applications and Trends***
- Very good introduction in SAR image formation from a CS and DL perspective.
- Present loop unrolling and model based deep learning ideas.
- Discusses challenges within the problem domain.

***Sparsity-Driven Synthetic Aperture Radar Imaging: Reconstruction, Autofocusing, Moving Targets and Compressed Sensing:***
- Provides an overview for compressed sensing used within SAR.
### UWB processing techniques 

***Performance Evaluation of Frequency Domain Algorithms for Chirped Low Frequency UWB SAR Data Processing***
- Standard chirp scaling cannot provide suitable results due to approximations, fixed with non-linear chirp scaling algorithms.
- Formulates UWB SAR model and shows coupling.
- Introduces and derives new algorithms.

***A Comparison Between Fast Factorised Backprojection and Frrequency Domain Algorithms in UWB Low Frequency SAR***
- Computational complexity of the algorithms.
- Discarding higher order terms in derivations.
- Approximations in traditional chirp scaling.
